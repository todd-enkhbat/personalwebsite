Blending Perspectives

Superposition

Just as a quantum particle exists in multiple states until observed, humanity's story can be viewed through multiple lenses — scientific and poetic — simultaneously.

Only by blending the reductionist, humanist, philosophical, and cosmological accounts can we grasp meaning in an impermanent universe.

Toggle Perspective

In the beginning, a singularity of infinite density expanded — energy cooling into matter, quarks forming hadrons, nucleosynthesis yielding hydrogen and helium. Across 13.8 billion years, gravitational collapse ignited stars, whose supernovae scattered heavier elements. On a rocky planet orbiting a G-type star, autocatalytic chemistry became self-replicating molecules, and evolution's algorithm iterated toward consciousness.

In the beginning, there was darkness — until a burst of light set the cosmos ablaze. From that first dawn, the universe dreamed itself into being: stars were born and died like fireflies in an infinite night. On a small blue world, something miraculous stirred in the waters — life reached toward the light, and after eons of striving, looked up at the stars that made it and asked: "Why?"
